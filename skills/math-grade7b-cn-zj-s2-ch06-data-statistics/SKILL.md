# Chapter 6: Data and Statistical Charts

## Use when

- The learner asks about collecting data, organizing data, reading or choosing charts, frequency, relative frequency, or histograms.

## Do not use when

- The topic is advanced probability or statistics outside this chapter.
- The learner only needs generic explanation, quiz generation, or answer checking; fallback to common Skills.

## Required references

Use all chapter reference files, especially `chapter-map.yaml`, `knowledge-points.yaml`, `assessment-items.yaml`, and `coverage-matrix.yaml`.

## Teaching procedure

1. Identify the data question and the data type.
2. Decide whether the task is collect, organize, chart, or interpret.
3. Choose the chart type based on what comparison or trend is needed.
4. Ask the learner to make one conclusion supported by the data.

## Diagnosis and remediation procedure

1. If chart choice is wrong, ask whether the data goal is comparison, change, part-whole, or grouped distribution.
2. If frequency and relative frequency are confused, require count over total before simplifying.
3. If a conclusion is unsupported, ask the learner to point to the exact data evidence.

Use items or variants from task templates.
