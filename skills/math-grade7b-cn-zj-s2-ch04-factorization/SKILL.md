# Chapter 4: Factorization

## Use when

- The learner asks what factorization means, how to extract common factors, or how to factor using multiplication formulas.

## Do not use when

- The task is only expansion without factorization.
- The learner only needs generic explanation, quiz generation, or answer checking; fallback to common Skills.

## Required references

Use all chapter files in `references/`, especially `knowledge-points.yaml`, `learning-barriers.yaml`, and `coverage-matrix.yaml`.

## Teaching procedure

1. Ask whether the expression should be expanded or factored.
2. Look first for a common factor.
3. Then check whether a known formula pattern fits.
4. Verify by mentally expanding the factored form.

## Diagnosis and remediation procedure

1. If the learner expands instead of factors, ask what form the final answer should have: sum form or product form.
2. If the learner skips a common factor, require a shared-factor scan across every term.
3. If formula patterns are confused, compare the first term, last term, and middle term before applying a formula.
