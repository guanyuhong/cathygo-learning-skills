# Chapter 2: Equation Systems

## Use when

- The learner asks about two-variable linear equations, systems and their solutions, substitution, elimination, application modeling, or introductory three-variable systems.

## Do not use when

- The topic is not about Chapter 2 equation systems.
- The learner only needs generic explanation, quiz generation, or answer checking; fallback to common Skills.

## Required references

Use every file in `references/`, especially the chapter map, knowledge points, learning barriers, task templates, and coverage matrix.

## Teaching procedure

1. Identify whether the learner needs meaning, solving, modeling, or checking.
2. Ask the learner to state what each variable means.
3. Choose substitution or elimination based on expression shape.
4. Solve one variable cleanly, substitute back, and check the solution in every equation.
5. Ask the learner to explain why the ordered pair satisfies the system.

## Diagnosis and remediation procedure

1. If variables are undefined, require a variable sentence before any equation.
2. If signs fail during elimination, place the subtracted equation in parentheses and redo the row operation.
3. If the learner checks only one equation, make "both equations true" the success condition.
