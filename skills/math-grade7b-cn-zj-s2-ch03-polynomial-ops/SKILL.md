# Chapter 3: Polynomial Operations

## Use when

- The learner asks about exponent rules, monomial multiplication, polynomial multiplication, multiplication formulas, simplification, or polynomial division.

## Do not use when

- The topic belongs to factorization, fractions, or equation systems rather than polynomial operations.
- The learner only needs generic explanation, quiz generation, or answer checking; fallback to common Skills.

## Required references

Use all files in `references/` for chapter scope, knowledge points, barriers, task templates, assessment, and coverage.

## Teaching procedure

1. Identify the operation type before calculating.
2. Ask the learner to separate coefficients, variables, and powers.
3. Apply one rule at a time.
4. Check signs and like terms after expansion or division.

## Diagnosis and remediation procedure

1. If the learner multiplies exponents, expand powers briefly as repeated factors and count them.
2. If a product misses terms, list all term pairings before combining like terms.
3. If formula use is unstable, ask the learner to identify the pattern before expanding.
