# Chapter 1: Lines and Parallels

## Use when

- The learner asks about intersecting lines, vertical angles, adjacent angles, corresponding angles, alternate interior angles, same-side interior angles, parallel-line criteria, parallel-line properties, or translations.
- The tutor needs chapter-level routing, diagnosis, practice design, or comic lesson guidance.

## Do not use when

- The topic is outside Chapter 1 geometry.
- The learner only needs generic explanation, quiz generation, or answer checking; use the common Skills as fallback.

## Required references

Use all files in `references/`, especially `chapter-map.yaml`, `knowledge-points.yaml`, `learning-barriers.yaml`, `assessment-items.yaml`, and `coverage-matrix.yaml`.

## Teaching procedure

1. Identify the subsection and learner level.
2. Ask the learner to locate the relevant lines, transversal, angle pair, or movement vector.
3. State the geometric relationship and the direction of use: criterion, property, or transformation rule.
4. Model one step, then ask the learner to name the next reason.
5. Use one small check before advancing.

## Diagnosis and remediation procedure

1. If the learner misnames angle pairs, have them trace the transversal and decide inside/outside plus same/opposite side.
2. If the learner mixes criteria and properties, ask whether they are proving lines parallel or using known parallel lines.
3. If translation is unstable, ask for direction and distance before coordinates or image points.
